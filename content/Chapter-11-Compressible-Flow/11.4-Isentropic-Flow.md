# Section 11.4: Isentropic Flow of an Ideal Gas

> **Core theme:** When flow is steady, one-dimensional, frictionless, and adiabatic, entropy is constant. The combination of the continuity, momentum, and energy equations with isentropic relations produces a differential equation linking velocity change to area change — the key to understanding how subsonic and supersonic flows respond differently to duct geometry.

***

## 1. Governing Equations for Steady 1-D Isentropic Flow

| Law | Equation |
|-----|----------|
| **Continuity** | $\dot{m} = \rho V A = \text{const}$ |
| **Energy (adiabatic)** | $h + V^2/2 = h_0 = \text{const}$ |
| **Isentropic** | $p/\rho^k = \text{const}$, $T/\rho^{k-1} = \text{const}$ |
| **Ideal gas law** | $p = \rho RT$ |

These four relationships fully determine the flow state at any station given geometry and boundary conditions.

***

## 2. The Area-Velocity Relation

The key differential equation of isentropic duct flow:

$$\frac{dV}{V} = -\frac{dA/A}{1 - \text{Ma}^2} \qquad \text{(11.44)}$$

Derived by differentiating the continuity equation ($d\rho/\rho + dV/V + dA/A = 0$) and substituting the isentropic relation ($dp = c^2 d\rho$) and Euler's equation ($dp = -\rho V dV$).

### 2.1 Physical Interpretation

| Regime | $\text{Ma} < 1$ (subsonic) | $\text{Ma} > 1$ (supersonic) |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| $dA < 0$ (converging) | $dV > 0$ (accelerates) | $dV < 0$ (decelerates) |
| $dA > 0$ (diverging) | $dV < 0$ (decelerates) | $dV > 0$ (accelerates) |
| $dA = 0$ (throat) | Ma can change gradually | Ma can change gradually |

> 📌 **Subsonic**: converging → faster, diverging → slower (same as incompressible intuition)
> 📌 **Supersonic**: converging → slower, diverging → faster (opposite to intuition!)
> 📌 **Sonic (Ma = 1)**: the denominator $1 - \text{Ma}^2 = 0$, so $dA = 0$ is required — **sonic flow can only occur at a throat** (minimum area)

---

## 3. Mass Flow Rate

From $\dot{m} = \rho VA$, substitute stagnation relations:

$$\dot{m} = \rho_0 A \text{Ma} \sqrt{kRT_0} \left(1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2\right)^{-\frac{k+1}{2(k-1)}} \qquad \text{(10.48)}$$

### 3.1 Maximum Mass Flow (Choked Flow)

For given stagnation conditions $(p_0, T_0)$, the mass flow rate reaches a **maximum** at Ma = 1 (the throat):

$$\dot{m}_{\text{max}} = A^* p_0 \sqrt{\frac{k}{RT_0}} \left(\frac{2}{k+1}\right)^{\frac{k+1}{2(k-1)}} \qquad \text{(11.58)}$$

For air ($k = 1.4$):
$$\dot{m}_{\text{max}} = 0.6847 \frac{A^* p_0}{\sqrt{R T_0}}$$

---

## 4. Area Ratio — The Isentropic Flow Relation

The ratio of area $A$ at any Mach number to the throat area $A^*$ (where Ma = 1) is:

$$\frac{A}{A^*} = \frac{1}{\text{Ma}}\left[\frac{2}{k+1}\left(1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2\right)\right]^{\frac{k+1}{2(k-1)}} \qquad \text{(11.59)}$$

| Ma (subsonic) | $A/A^*$ | Ma (supersonic) | $A/A^*$ |
|:-------------:|:-------:|:---------------:|:-------:|
| 0.1 | 5.822 | 1.0 | 1.000 |
| 0.2 | 2.964 | 1.5 | 1.176 |
| 0.3 | 2.035 | 2.0 | 1.688 |
| 0.5 | 1.340 | 2.5 | 2.637 |
| 0.7 | 1.094 | 3.0 | 4.235 |
| 0.9 | 1.009 | 5.0 | 25.00 |

> 📌 **Key observation:** A given $A/A^*$ can correspond to **two Mach numbers** — one subsonic and one supersonic (except at the throat itself). The same geometry can produce very different flows depending on back pressure conditions.

---

## 5. Convergent-Divergent (C-D) Nozzle

The C-D nozzle (de Laval nozzle) is the classic isentropic flow device:

1. Subsonic flow enters the convergent section and accelerates
2. Ma = 1 at the throat (if back pressure is low enough)
3. Supersonic flow in the divergent section further accelerates

**Without a divergent section**: sonic velocity is the maximum achievable in a simple converging nozzle.

**With a divergent section**: supersonic velocities are achievable — every rocket engine and supersonic wind tunnel uses a C-D nozzle.

---

## 6. Using Isentropic Tables

In practice, engineers use tabulated "Isentropic Flow Tables" instead of Eq. (11.59) for each calculation:

| Given | Read from table |
|-------|----------------|
| Ma | $T/T_0$, $p/p_0$, $\rho/\rho_0$, $A/A^*$ |
| $A/A^*$ | Two possible Ma values (subsonic & supersonic) |
| $p/p_0$ | Ma, then other ratios |

---

## Key Takeaways

| Concept | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| $dV/V = -(dA/A)/(1 - \text{Ma}^2)$ | The fundamental law of compressible duct flow — explains all subsonic/supersonic reversal behavior |
| Ma = 1 only at a throat | Sonic condition requires a minimum area |
| Two Mach numbers per $A/A^*$ | The same nozzle can operate subsonically or supersonically depending on back pressure |
| Choked mass flow depends only on $p_0$, $T_0$, $A^*$ | Once choked, decreasing back pressure increases neither Mach number nor mass flow |
