# Section 7.5: Similitude and Modeling

> **Core theme:** Similitude is the science of relating model-test results to full-scale prototype behavior. Three types of similarity must be satisfied, and complete similarity is often impossible — requiring **distorted models** and engineering judgment.

***

## 1. The Modeling Problem

**Goal:** Predict prototype (full-scale) behavior from model (laboratory-scale) measurements.

**Common applications:**
- Ship hulls in towing tanks
- Aircraft wings in wind tunnels
- Hydraulic structures (dams, spillways, levees)
- Automobile aerodynamics

***

## 2. Three Types of Similarity

### Geometric Similarity

| Aspect | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Model and prototype have the **same shape** — all linear dimensions scaled by constant factor $\lambda_L$ |
| **Math** | $\dfrac{L_m}{L_p} = \lambda_L$ (constant for all dimensions) |
| **Also** | Angles are identical; surface roughness scaled (hard!) |
| **Check** | If model is 1:50 scale, every length is 1/50 of prototype |

### Kinematic Similarity

| Aspect | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Flow streamlines are geometrically similar — velocities scale by constant factor |
| **Math** | $\dfrac{V_m}{V_p} = \lambda_V$ at all corresponding points |
| **Result** | Ratios of velocities, accelerations are constant throughout |
| **Check** | Pathlines and streamlines have the same shape |

### Dynamic Similarity

| Aspect | Condition |
|--------|-----------|
| **Definition** | Ratios of forces (inertia, viscous, gravity, pressure, surface tension) are the same |
| **Math** | Corresponding dimensionless groups match: $\text{Re}_m = \text{Re}_p$, $\text{Fr}_m = \text{Fr}_p$, etc. |
| **Check** | The force polygons at corresponding points are geometrically similar |

> **Complete similarity requires all three** — but this is often impossible in practice.

---

## 3. The Scaling Dilemma

### Why Complete Similarity Is Hard

To satisfy **both** Reynolds and Froude similarity simultaneously:

| Condition | Equation |
|-----------|----------|
| Reynolds: | $\text{Re}_m = \text{Re}_p \;\rightarrow\; \dfrac{\rho_m V_m L_m}{\mu_m} = \dfrac{\rho_p V_p L_p}{\mu_p}$ |
| Froude: | $\text{Fr}_m = \text{Fr}_p \;\rightarrow\; \dfrac{V_m}{\sqrt{g L_m}} = \dfrac{V_p}{\sqrt{g L_p}}$ |

If scale is $\lambda_L = L_m/L_p$ and same fluid ($\rho_m = \rho_p$, $\mu_m = \mu_p$):

From Froude: $V_m = V_p \sqrt{\lambda_L}$
From Reynolds: $V_m = V_p / \lambda_L$

For $\lambda_L \neq 1$, these are contradictory unless the fluid is changed.

### Possible Solutions

| Approach | How | Trade-off |
|----------|-----|-----------|
| **Same fluid, Froude scaling** | Match $\text{Fr}$, accept $\text{Re}$ mismatch | Viscous effects distorted |
| **Different fluid** | Choose fluid with appropriate $\rho/\mu$ ratio | May be impractical or expensive |
| **Distorted model** | Different horizontal/vertical scales | Analysis more complex |
| **Numerical complement** | Use model data + CFD to correct scaling errors | Requires validation |

## 4. Prediction Equation Structure

Once similarity is achieved (or approximated), dimensionless groups from the model apply to the prototype:

$$\Pi_{\text{model}} = \Pi_{\text{prototype}}$$

**Example — Drag on a body:**
$$\frac{F_D}{\rho V^2 L^2} = \phi(\text{Re}, \text{Fr}, \text{Ma}, \ldots)$$

If $\text{Re}_m = \text{Re}_p$ and $\text{Fr}_m = \text{Fr}_p$, then:

$$\frac{F_{D,m}}{\rho_m V_m^2 L_m^2} = \frac{F_{D,p}}{\rho_p V_p^2 L_p^2}$$

Solve for $F_{D,p}$:
$$F_{D,p} = F_{D,m} \cdot \frac{\rho_p}{\rho_m} \cdot \left(\frac{V_p}{V_m}\right)^2 \cdot \left(\frac{L_p}{L_m}\right)^2$$

---

## 5. Distorted Models

When complete similarity is impossible, we use **distorted models**:
- Different horizontal scale ($\lambda_H$) and vertical scale ($\lambda_V$)
- Used primarily in free-surface flows (rivers, harbors, estuaries)
- Must carefully reinterpret results

---

## Example: Katrina Levee Model

> The U.S. Army Corps of Engineers built a 1:50 scale model of the 17th Street Outfall Canal (New Orleans) covering 15,000 sq ft, replicating 0.5 mile of canal and >1 mile of Lake Pontchartrain front. Computer-controlled wave generators produced varying wave heights/periods/directions matching Hurricane Katrina conditions.

## Key Takeaways

| Concept | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Geometric + Kinematic + Dynamic similarity | All three needed for **complete** similitude |
| $\text{Re}$ and $\text{Fr}$ conflict in scaling | You often must choose which dominates |
| Prediction equations use $\Pi$ groups | $\Pi_{\text{model}} = \Pi_{\text{prototype}}$ |
| Distorted models are common | Engineering judgment is part of the process |
