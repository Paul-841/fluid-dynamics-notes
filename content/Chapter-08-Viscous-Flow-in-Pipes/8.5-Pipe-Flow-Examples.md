# Section 8.5: Pipe Flow Examples

> **Core theme:** The energy equation with friction losses is the workhorse for designing real pipe systems. Three problem types emerge — find $\Delta p$, find $Q$, or find $D$ — each requiring a different solution strategy.

***

## 1. The Extended Bernoulli Equation with Losses

For steady, incompressible flow between sections (1) and (2):

$$\frac{p_1}{\gamma} + \frac{V_1^2}{2g} + z_1 + h_p = \frac{p_2}{\gamma} + \frac{V_2^2}{2g} + z_2 + h_t + h_L$$

where:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| $h_p$ | **Pump head** added (energy in) |
| $h_t$ | **Turbine head** extracted (energy out) |
| $h_L$ | **Total head loss** = major + minor |

$$h_L = h_{L,\text{major}} + h_{L,\text{minor}}$$

***

## 2. Major Losses — Straight Pipe Friction

**Darcy–Weisbach equation:**

$$h_{L,\text{major}} = f\,\frac{\ell}{D}\,\frac{V^2}{2g}$$

$f$ from Moody chart or Colebrook.

| Problem Type | Known | Find | Strategy |
|-------------|-------|------|----------|
| **Type I** | $Q$, $D$, $\varepsilon$ | $h_L$ ($\Delta p$) | Direct: $\text{Re}$ → $f$ → $h_L$ |
| **Type II** | $D$, $\varepsilon$, $h_L$ | $Q$ | Iterative: guess $f$ → $V$ → $\text{Re}$ → $f_{\text{new}}$ |
| **Type III** | $Q$, $\varepsilon$, $h_L$ | $D$ | Iterative: guess $D$ → $V$ → $\text{Re}$ → $f$ → $D_{\text{new}}$ |

### Type I — Direct Solution
1. $V = 4Q/(\pi D^2)$
2. $\text{Re} = \rho V D / \mu$
3. Get $f$ from Moody chart / Colebrook
4. $h_L = f(\ell/D)(V^2/2g)$

### Type II — Find Flowrate (Iterative)
1. Guess $f$ (e.g., $f = 0.02$ for turbulent)
2. Solve energy eqn for $V$
3. Compute $\text{Re}$, then $f_{\text{new}}$ from Colebrook
4. If $|f_{\text{new}} - f| >$ tolerance, go to step 2

> 💡 **Convergence tip:** Colebrook is well-behaved — 2-3 iterations usually converge.

### Type III — Find Diameter (Iterative)
1. Guess $D$
2. Compute $V$, $\text{Re}$, $f$ from Colebrook
3. Solve energy eqn for $D_{\text{new}}$
4. If $|D_{\text{new}} - D| >$ tolerance, go to step 1

***

## 3. Minor Losses — Fittings and Geometry Changes

$$h_{L,\text{minor}} = K_L \frac{V^2}{2g}$$

| Component | $K_L$ (typical) |
|-----------|----------------|
| Sharp entrance | 0.5 |
| Well-rounded entrance | 0.04–0.1 |
| Sharp exit | 1.0 |
| 90° elbow, threaded | 1.5 |
| 90° elbow, flanged | 0.3 |
| 45° elbow | 0.4 |
| Gate valve (fully open) | 0.15 |
| Globe valve (fully open) | 10 |
| Tee (branch flow) | 1.0–2.0 |

> 📌 Minor losses are **not always minor** — a partly closed globe valve ($K_L \approx 10$) can dominate the system head loss.

**Equivalent length method:** $h_L = f(L_e/D)(V^2/2g)$ where $L_e$ is tabulated.

***

## 4. Pumps and Turbines

### Pump Power
$$P_{\text{pump}} = \frac{\gamma Q h_p}{\eta_p}$$

where $\eta_p$ = pump efficiency (typically 0.6–0.9).

### Turbine Power
$$P_{\text{turbine}} = \gamma Q h_t \,\eta_t$$

where $\eta_t$ = turbine efficiency.

***

## 5. Single Pipe System Solution Process

1. Draw system, label sections (1) and (2)
2. Write energy equation with all terms
3. Determine flow regime ($\text{Re}$ → laminar or turbulent)
4. Get $f$ (formula or Moody chart)
5. Compute major + minor losses
6. Solve for unknown ($\Delta p$, $Q$, $D$, or pump head)
