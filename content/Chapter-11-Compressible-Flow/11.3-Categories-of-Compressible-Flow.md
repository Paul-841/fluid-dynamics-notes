# Section 11.3: Categories of Compressible Flow & Stagnation Conditions

> **Core theme:** Before solving compressible flow problems, we need to classify the type of flow and understand stagnation (or "total") conditions — the state a fluid would attain if brought to rest isentropically. Stagnation conditions are the natural reference state for compressible flow.

***

## 1. Incompressible vs Compressible Criterion

From Bernoulli's equation extended to compressible flow, the percentage change in density can be approximated:

$$\frac{\Delta\rho}{\rho} \approx \frac{1}{2}\text{Ma}^2$$

- For Ma = 0.3: $\Delta\rho/\rho \approx 4.5\%$ — the commonly accepted threshold
- Below Ma = 0.3: use incompressible methods
- Above Ma = 0.3: compressibility effects must be included

***

## 2. Stagnation (Total) Conditions

### 2.1 Definition

**Stagnation conditions** $(p_0, T_0, \rho_0)$ are the properties a flowing fluid would have if it were brought to **rest** ($V = 0$) **isentropically** (reversibly and adiabatically).

- $p_0$ = stagnation (total) pressure
- $T_0$ = stagnation (total) temperature
- $\rho_0$ = stagnation density

> ⚠️ **Important:** Stagnation is an *idealized* process. Real deceleration involves irreversibilities (friction, shocks) — the actual stagnation pressure is lower.

### 2.2 Derivation from Energy Equation

For steady, adiabatic, inviscid flow of an ideal gas, the energy equation (first law) gives:

$$h_1 + \frac{V_1^2}{2} = h_2 + \frac{V_2^2}{2} = h_0 \qquad \text{(constant along streamline)}$$

For an ideal gas with constant $c_p$: $h = c_p T$

$$c_p T + \frac{V^2}{2} = c_p T_0 \qquad \text{(11.37)}$$

Rearranging:

$$T_0 = T + \frac{V^2}{2c_p} \qquad \text{(11.38)}$$

Using $c_p = kR/(k-1)$ and $c = \sqrt{kRT}$, $\text{Ma} = V/c$:

### 2.3 Stagnation Temperature Ratio

$$\boxed{\frac{T_0}{T} = 1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2} \qquad \text{(11.40)}$$

For **incompressible** flow (Ma → 0): $T_0 \approx T$ — temperature changes due to kinetic energy conversion are negligible.

### 2.4 Stagnation Pressure & Density

Using the isentropic relations (Eqs. 11.22):

$$\boxed{\frac{p_0}{p} = \left(1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2\right)^{k/(k-1)}} \qquad \text{(11.41)}$$

$$\boxed{\frac{\rho_0}{\rho} = \left(1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2\right)^{1/(k-1)}} \qquad \text{(11.42)}$$

### 2.5 Physical Interpretation

| Ma | $p_0/p$ (air, $k=1.4$) | $T_0/T$ |
|----|----------------------|---------|
| 0.0 | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| 0.3 | 1.064 | 1.018 |
| 0.5 | 1.186 | 1.050 |
| 0.8 | 1.524 | 1.128 |
| 1.0 | 1.893 | 1.200 |
| 2.0 | 7.824 | 1.800 |
| 3.0 | 36.73 | 2.800 |

At higher Mach numbers, stagnation pressure grows enormously — a key reason why Pitot tubes need compressibility corrections at high speeds.

---

## 3. Weakest Link: The Single Energy Equation

The energy equation $h + V^2/2 = \text{const}$ is **valid along a streamline for steady, adiabatic, inviscid flow, or for any flow through a control volume with one inlet and one outlet under steady, adiabatic conditions, regardless of irreversibilities inside**.

- It does NOT require isentropic flow
- It does NOT require frictionless flow
- It only requires adiabatic, steady conditions

This is the **most robust** of all compressible flow equations.

---

## 4. Critical Conditions (Ma = 1)

The "**critical**" or "**sonic**" state (Ma = 1, denoted by superscript $^*$) is a special reference:

- $T^*/T_0 = 2/(k+1)$
- $p^*/p_0 = [2/(k+1)]^{k/(k-1)}$
- $\rho^*/\rho_0 = [2/(k+1)]^{1/(k-1)}$

For air ($k = 1.4$):
************

| Critical ratio  | Value  |
| --------------- | ------ |
| $T^*/T_0$       | 0.8333 |
| $p^*/p_0$       | 0.5283 |
| $\rho^*/\rho_0$ | 0.6339 |

> 📌 **$p^*/p_0 = 0.528$ for air** — this is a key number. When the pressure ratio across a converging nozzle drops below 0.528, the flow at the throat reaches Mach 1 and becomes "choked."

---

## Key Takeaways

| Concept | Why It Matters |
|---------|---------------|
| Ma < 0.3 → incompressible | Saves you from doing full compressible analysis when it's not needed |
| $T_0/T = 1 + \frac{k-1}{2}\text{Ma}^2$ | Universal relation for adiabatic steady flow |
| $p_0/p$ from isentropic relation | Only valid for isentropic flow (not across shocks) |
| Critical $p^*/p_0 = 0.528$ | Determines choking condition for air in converging nozzles |
