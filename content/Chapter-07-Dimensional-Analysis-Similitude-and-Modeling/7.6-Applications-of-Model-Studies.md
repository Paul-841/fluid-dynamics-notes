# Section 7.6: Applications of Model Studies

> **Core theme:** The ultimate goal of dimensional analysis and similitude is **practical prediction**. This section walks through real engineering applications where model studies provide essential design data.

***

## 1. Pipe Flow (Friction Factor Correlation)

### Classic Example — Moody Chart

The Moody chart is the result of dimensional analysis applied to pipe flow:

$$\frac{\Delta p}{\frac{1}{2}\rho V^2} \cdot \frac{D}{L} = f = \phi\!\left(\text{Re},\ \frac{\varepsilon}{D}\right)$$

where:
- $f$ = Darcy friction factor (dimensionless)
- $\text{Re} = \rho V D / \mu$ = Reynolds number
- $\varepsilon/D$ = relative roughness (dimensionless)

**Key insight:** The entire Moody chart (smooth to fully rough, laminar to turbulent) was produced from laboratory experiments on small pipes and generalized to all pipe scales via dimensional analysis.

| Regime | Functional Form |
|--------|----------------|
| Laminar | $f = 64 / \text{Re}$ |
| Turbulent smooth | $1/\sqrt{f} \approx 2.0 \log_{10}(\text{Re}\sqrt{f}) - 0.8$ |
| Turbulent fully rough | $1/\sqrt{f} = 2.0 \log_{10}(3.7D/\varepsilon)$ |

## 2. Drag on Bodies (External Flows)

Drag coefficient is a dimensionless form of the drag force:

$$C_D = \frac{F_D}{\frac{1}{2}\rho V^2 A}$$

From dimensional analysis:

$$C_D = \phi(\text{Re}, \text{Ma}, \text{Fr}, \text{shape}, \text{roughness}, \ldots)$$

### Modeling Strategy

| Application | Primary Similarity | Secondary |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Submarine (deep) | $\text{Re}$ | — |
| Ship (surface) | $\text{Fr}$ | $\text{Re}$ |
| Aircraft (low-speed) | $\text{Re}$ | — |
| Aircraft (high-speed) | $\text{Ma}$, $\text{Re}$ | — |

## 3. Hydraulic Structures

### Open Channel / Spillway Models

For a dam spillway, the Froude number governs because gravity drives the flow:

$$\text{Fr}_m = \text{Fr}_p \;\rightarrow\; \frac{V_m}{\sqrt{g L_m}} = \frac{V_p}{\sqrt{g L_p}}$$

### Discharge Scaling

Model discharge $Q_m$ relates to prototype $Q_p$:

$$Q \propto V L^2 \propto \sqrt{g L} \cdot L^2 = g^{1/2} L^{5/2}$$

Under Froude scaling ($V \propto \sqrt{L}$):
$$\frac{Q_m}{Q_p} = \lambda_L^{5/2}$$

### Force Scaling

Pressure and force scale as:
$$\frac{F_m}{F_p} = \lambda_L^3$$

## 4. Pumps and Fans (Hydraulic Machinery)

**Specific speed** is a dimensionless group used to classify turbomachinery:

$$N_s = \frac{N \sqrt{Q}}{(g H)^{3/4}}$$

| $N_s$ Range | Pump Type |
|------------|-----------|
| < 500 | Radial / centrifugal |
| 500–4000 | Mixed flow |
| > 4000 | Axial flow |

Pump performance maps are made dimensionless using:
- **Flow coefficient:** $\phi = Q / (N D^3)$
- **Head coefficient:** $\psi = g H / (N^2 D^2)$
- **Power coefficient:** $\Pi_P = P / (\rho N^3 D^5)$

**Affinity Laws** (from dimensional analysis):
$$\frac{Q_1}{Q_2} = \frac{N_1}{N_2}, \qquad \frac{H_1}{H_2} = \left(\frac{N_1}{N_2}\right)^2, \qquad \frac{P_1}{P_2} = \left(\frac{N_1}{N_2}\right)^3$$

## 5. Wind Engineering / Building Aerodynamics

Scale models of buildings in wind tunnels:
- Match $\text{Re}$ (or accept mismatch and correct)
- Match $\text{Ma}$ if compressibility matters
- Model atmospheric boundary layer velocity profile

**Common applications:**
- Pedestrian-level wind comfort
- Structural wind loads
- Ventilation and exhaust dispersion
- Bridge deck flutter (aeroelastic models)

## Key Takeaways

| Application | Key $\Pi$ Group(s) | What's Predicted |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Pipe flow | $\text{Re}, \varepsilon/D$ | Pressure drop, friction factor |
| Drag | $\text{Re}, \text{Ma}$ | Drag force |
| Spillway | $\text{Fr}$ | Discharge, water surface profile |
| Ship resistance | $\text{Fr}, \text{Re}$ | Total drag, power requirement |
| Pump/fan | $N_s, \phi, \psi$ | Performance curves |
| Building aerodynamics | $\text{Re}$, wind profile | Pressure distribution, loads |
